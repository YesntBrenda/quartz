
I'd like there to be some kind of connection between this universe and the ones of prior campaigns. Being in the exact same one would be limiting so I'm currently thinking of implementing a multiversal relationship. This would allow me to summon former player characters to use as encounters or NPCs to help progress the story. More importantly, it would mean that I DON'T HAVE TO CREATE MANY OF MY OWN CHARACTERS. HECK YEAH.

### Multiversal Aspect
- Notes
	- An individual person causing the multiversal collisions would make for a very definitive *Big Bad*. The problems with this would be that I then have to make it seem like they are active at the same time as our party. Given the large power gap between them, this could cause issues both in scaling as well as on my part to plan their actions in feeling cohesive while not ruining the campaign.
	- Alternatively, it could be the work of a malicious organization that results in the shattering of multiversal boundaries. I like this approach because it allows for the main antagonists to be threatening while not in total control of the multiversal anomalies that take place.
	- Final approach that I'm open to would be that it's the work of a god. Joker and Joaquin were very prominent deity figures of chaos in the first three campaigns, so incorporating some lore surrounding them to initiate the multiversal chaos would be a familiar concept. Joker's dead and Joaquin still has "developing lore" so I don't think it would be smart to build upon stuff that hasn't been resolved. Going on the DC theme, I think using the name "Barbatos" would be good, as it sounds intimidating, is the name of a demon, and also is the name of a DC villain with strong ties to the multiverse. This still suffers from the same concerns of the first proposition though, as issues arrive surrounding the party's ability to combat a god of unfathomable power set on their demise.

### Barbatos
- Notes
	- I think I've decided that Barbatos will not necessarily be the antagonist, but rather the vessel that the evil organization uses to further their goals. What exactly the cult using Barbatos wishes to see is something I've yet to decide on.
	- I think Barbatos should be a god of 'Other Fates' or 'Alternate Paths'. This would grant him the ability to transport things from alternate timelines/universes into the desired one, and perhaps allow for some interesting interactions with the party around fate itself.
	- Barbatos should have some sort of dichotomy with Savras, god of Divination and Fate. Perhaps Barbatos himself is an "Other Fate" for Savras, and this relationship drives his willingness to work with the cult. Perhaps out of jealousy for his domain, or pity for the 'Other Fates' that never realized.
	- At some point I'd like a neutral interaction with the party and Barbatos, where he can reveal his intentions and why he does what the cult asks of him.
	- On further thought, it might be a good idea to make Barbatos less personally involved in the matter by making him the patron deity of one person—or perhaps a sect of people who happen to know of his otherwise secret existence. I'm personally leaning towards the cult interpretation, because that allows me to establish that the most evangelical of a religious order are granted power from their god, establishing their kinghood over their designated area. Each king would act as a sort of demigod pastor, and perhaps make for very interesting bosses in the future.

### World Dynamics
- Notes
	- Each nation is headed not by a political king, but a God King. These are the most dedicated followers of a particular god, and through their dedication and evangelizing they've created nations of followers who bolster their own abilities granted by their respective patron deities.
	- Some nations coexist while others are at eternal war due to their conflicting ideals. Not all gods have nations, but most have at least a city, and even the evil ones have at least organizations that pay worship to them.
	- Gods/Goddesses that I may want to preside over countries:
		- Azuth god of Wizards, Mystra goddess of Magic, Chauntea goddess of Agriculture, Gond god of Craft, Helm god of Protection, Mielikki goddess of Forests, Silvanus god of Wild Nature, Oghma god of Knowledge, Savras god of Divination and Fate, Sune goddess of Love and Beauty, Torm god of Courage and Self-Sacrifice, Tymora goddess of Luck, Waukeen goddess of trade
	- Gods/Goddesses that I want for more specialized roles:
		- Eldath goddess of Peace: Demigod is head of International Peacekeeping Body
		- Ilmater god of Endurance: Demigod is head of mail service
		- Kelemvor god of the Dead: Demigod is overseer of all morticians
		- Leira goddess of Illusion: "WANNA SEE A MAGIC TRICK???"
		- Tyr god of Justice: Demigods compose a supreme court
	- Due to this world's heavy fixation on religion and the manifestation of their respective gods' abilities, the peacekeeping body of Ilmater found a joint resolution between all of the major gods to allow the worshipping of up to 3 deities: The one of their country, the one of their profession, and the one of their race. Worshipping multiple would decrease their granted power (as to encourage total dedication) but it would not be a punishable offense.
	- Just by the nature of some of the gods/goddesses domains, there should be an upper circle of monarchs that are revered by the lower circle kings. I for sure want Savras' god king to be of the upper circle, and I'll decide on the rest of their places later.
	- I also want the kings/queens to be active within their kingdoms whenever possible. It would be pointless to be granted the most ability of their realm, yet be reserved to just sitting on a throne all day. For example, the god king of Azuth would be in control of the largest teleportation gateways, or be the one to cast the protective barrier around the city.

- ### Planescape Integration
